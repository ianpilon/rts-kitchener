Refrigeration, plainly

When it's hotter outside, your reefer cools less.

It feels backwards. Most people assume that on a hot day the unit should kick into a higher gear and pull harder. The physics works the other way. Drag the temperature below and watch what happens.

The myth

"It's hotter out, so it should cool harder."

The science

Hotter air → harder to dump heat → head pressure climbs → capacity drops.

Before the heat wave hits

A reefer isn't a cooler. It needs maintenance to fight rising ambient temps.

Clean condenser coils, proper refrigerant charge, tight belts, and serviced fans are the difference between holding temperature and losing your load.
